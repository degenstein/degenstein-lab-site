What is CoinDepo?

CoinDepo is a centralized yield platform designed for crypto holders who want supported assets to earn interest instead of remaining idle.

Rather than focusing on active trading, CoinDepo is structured around time-based yield products that reward patience, planning, and commitment.

Supported assets & yield ranges

CoinDepo supports a mix of major cryptocurrencies and stablecoins. Yield varies by asset type, term length, and product structure.

  • Major crypto assets (e.g., BTC, ETH, SOL, XRP)
  • Stablecoins (e.g., USDT, USDC) typically offer higher baseline yield
  • Yield commonly falls in the low-to-mid double-digit APR range

Rates are dynamic and should always be verified directly inside the CoinDepo app.

How yield products are structured

Yield on CoinDepo is primarily compensation for time and reduced liquidity. Higher advertised rates generally reflect longer commitments or stricter withdrawal rules.

  • Term-based deposits (e.g., 30, 90, 180, 365 days)
  • Simple APR vs compounded APY depending on product
  • Defined payout schedules and rollover mechanics

Security & custody model

CoinDepo operates as a custodial platform. Users should independently assess custody structure, internal controls, insurance representations, and jurisdictional exposure.

$COINDEPO Token Advantage

$COINDEPO is the native ecosystem token associated with the CoinDepo platform. It is designed to align long-term users with the platform’s growth rather than replace yield products.

Holding $COINDEPO may unlock platform-level advantages such as enhanced yield tiers, preferential product access, or other incentive structures as defined by CoinDepo. These benefits are optional and evolve over time.

Important distinction
  • Yield usage does not require holding the token
  • Token exposure introduces market price risk
  • Platform risk and token risk are not the same

Crypto-backed loans

Loans can provide liquidity without selling assets but introduce liquidation risk. Conservative usage and margin awareness are critical.

DYOR checklist

  • Understand lockups, exits, and rollover mechanics
  • Confirm APR vs APY and compounding frequency
  • Assess platform risk versus yield compensation
  • Never allocate funds you may need liquid

Final risk notes

Yield is compensation for constraints and risk. If you cannot explain the downside, you are not ready to deploy.
