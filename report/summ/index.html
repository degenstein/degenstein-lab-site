Overview

Summ is a crypto tax preparation platform designed to track, normalize, and reconcile on-chain and exchange activity into tax-ready reports. It is built for users operating across multiple wallets, chains, and protocols.

This is not a speculative product category. Summ exists because crypto activity has outgrown manual accounting. As participation scales, compliance becomes infrastructure.

How Crypto Tax Reporting Broke

Early crypto users could track activity with spreadsheets. Single wallets, limited exchanges, and infrequent trades made manual reporting possible.

That model collapsed with DeFi, bridges, wrapped assets, NFTs, and automated strategies. Users now generate thousands of taxable events without explicit buy or sell actions. Manual tracking no longer scales.

Who Summ Is Built For

  • • Active traders with frequent taxable events
  • • DeFi users interacting across chains
  • • Users managing multiple wallets and exchanges
  • • Individuals and entities required to file crypto taxes accurately

Who Summ Is Not For

  • • Buy-and-hold users with minimal activity
  • • Users unwilling to review edge cases
  • • Anyone seeking tax avoidance tools

Failure Modes & Limitations

No crypto tax platform eliminates complexity. Certain activities may require review, especially experimental protocols or non-standard transactions.

Why This Market Is Non-Cyclical

Compliance demand persists across bull and bear markets. Taxes are owed regardless of price action. This creates structural demand for tax infrastructure.

Long-Term Outlook (2026–2028)

As crypto adoption expands, reporting becomes automated, standardized, and enforced at the infrastructure layer. Platforms built for complexity will dominate.
